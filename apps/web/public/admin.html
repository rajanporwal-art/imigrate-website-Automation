Blog & News posts

Newest date shows first. Tick "Draft" to hide a post, or set a future date to schedule it. "Content" supports: blank line = new paragraph, "## " heading, "- " bullet, **bold**, [link](https://url).

FAQs

Edit the questions & answers shown on the FAQ page (and the "Popular questions" blocks). The Google rich-snippet schema rebuilds automatically. "Link to" / "Link label" are optional — they add a "Learn more" link under an answer (e.g. /canada-immigration).

Custom forms

Build forms that appear at /form/<slug>. Every submission is captured and auto-synced to HubSpot. Link to a form from a button, menu or blog post. Use the field "name" values in HubSpot field mapping to send them to dedicated properties.

HubSpot settings

Create a HubSpot form (with Email, First name, Last name, Phone and Message fields), then paste your Portal ID and that form's GUID here. Every website form will create/update a contact in HubSpot. Leads are also stored on your server, so nothing is lost if HubSpot is down. Click “Save changes” after editing settings.

Field mapping → HubSpot properties

Map a website field name (left) to a HubSpot contact property (right). Each mapped property must also exist as a field on your HubSpot form, or HubSpot will reject the submission. Full name is auto-split into firstname/lastname. Unmapped fields are still saved in the Message property.

Leads

Every submission from every form is captured here, even if HubSpot is unavailable.

Advanced — edit the full content file

For power users. Edit any field here as JSON. Must stay valid JSON (matching quotes/commas).

After saving, open your website and hard-refresh (Ctrl/Cmd+Shift+R). Previous version is backed up as content.backup.json.
